The History And Culture Of Honduras

Name, Location, and History

    Honduras is located in Central America and is bordered by Guatemela and Nicaragua

    The capital of Honduras is Tegucigalpa

    Choloma, Comayagua, an La Ceiba are also cities that are located in Honduras.

    Honduras was first sighted by Europeans when Christopher Columbus arrived at the Bay Islands on 30 July 1502.Columbus named the country Honduras ("depths") for the deep waters off its coast.

    The largest ethnic group in Honduras is the Garifuna people, who make up 55% of the population

    Spanish is the national language of Honduras

    The governmant of Honduras is Presidential/Constitutional Republic

Thomas Vincent Ramos

    Died: November 13, 1955, Dangriga, Belize

    As leader and spokesman of the Garifuna people, along with Pantaleon Hernandez and Domingo Ventura, Tomas approached the governor of the Wesleyan colony and asked for establishment of a public and bank holiday to observe the Garifuna arrival in Belize. The request was granted and the Carib Disembarkation Day, later named Garifuna Settlement Day, was first celebrated in Stann Creek District on November 19, 1941. The holiday was recognized in Toledo District in 1943 and throughout the country in 1977.

    Since 1956, a torchlight parade has been held on November 13 every year in his honour for his contributions to his Garifuna people. The parade is part of the week-long lead-up activities to the Garifuna Settlement Day on November 19. The celebration includes the selection of Miss Garifuna, parades and special church services, and the T. V. Ramos Classic Bike Race. His granddaughter Adele Ramos published a biography of T. V. Ramos in 2000. A statue of Thomas Vincent Ramos was unveiled in Dangriga Town in his honour.

Flag

    The blue stripes represent the Pacific Ocean and the Caribbean Sea. The five stars represent the five nations of the United States of Central America (Honduras, El Salvador, Costa Rica, Nicaragua, and Guatemala) and the hope that the nations may form a union again.

    The modern day design of this flag was established March 7, 1866

Food And Recreation

    Soccer, Tenis, and Baseball are three of the most common sports in Honduras.

    Carne Asada is a very popular dish, it is grilled meats marinated in lemon juice, cilantro, and other spices

    Baleadas are tortillas filled with cheese, beans,avacados, and ground beef

    Horchata is a kind of drink that has rice, water, cinnamon, sugar, vanilla and milk added afterward.
